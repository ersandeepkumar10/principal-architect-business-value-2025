Executive Summary

Purpose

To present data regarding a proposed reporting structure change and its potential organizational impact, based on quantitative metrics, stakeholder feedback, historical precedent, and business outcomes from 2025.

Current Situation

The Principal Architect covering 93% of platform architecture (14/15 business areas) and performing 6 organizational roles simultaneously has been placed to report to a Technical Architect covering 7% of the platform (1/15 business areas) and performing a single role.

Key Concerns

  • Coverage Disparity: 14:1 ratio in business area responsibility
  • Historical Failure: Same reporting structure tried previously (QA to US manager) resulted in quality degradation, visibility gaps, and undetected non-performance. When corrected (QA to India-based Principal), team transformed to high-performance
  • 2025 Performance: $1M+ business value, 550+ deliveries, 4 platform transformations, 11 proactive POCs‚Äîall in single year
  • Stakeholder Impact: December 2025 peer feedback from 8 departments describes Principal as "invaluable," "critical," someone organization is "truly lucky to have"
  • Timezone Constraints: 9.5-12.5 hour gap will recreate communication delays that previously caused documented failures

Business Impact at Risk

Innovation velocity, stakeholder relationships, team performance, and proactive problem-solving capability that generated $1M+ value in 2025 may be constrained. The organization has concrete evidence from previous QA reporting structure that this model creates bottlenecks and visibility failures.

Recommendation

Review reporting structure decision against data: coverage metrics, 2025 performance outcomes, stakeholder feedback, historical precedent, and timezone operational realities. Three alternative structures proposed that align scope with reporting authority.
